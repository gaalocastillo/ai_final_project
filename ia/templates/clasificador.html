Project:
Identifying social issues in online news using text classification

Introduction

Countries face different socio economic and political problems. Identifying those issues is crucial for governments and political parties to establish efficient plans to handle them. Currently, what governments do is to check the perception of their citizens through costly censuses. This is critical for undeveloped countries and countries in means of development, where resources are limited and must be managed very carefully. There are also independent and non-profit organizations like Latinobarómetro, that are dedicated to observe the development of democracies, economies and societies.
Latinobarómetro conducts surveys every year to identify the main problems Latin American nations face, based on public opinion. Nevertheless, these surveys are also costly and consequently, they cannot be carried out frequently.
Another conventional way to know about what happens in a country is by reading the news. With the expansion of the Internet, something that happens in a country miles away can be known almost in real time by anyone in any part of the world. Reading news may be useful to get brief information about the current state of a country, by providing necessary information about development, war, education etc [9]. Normally, news providers divide their headlines in different sections. Moreover, thanks to the Internet, these providers make available their articles through the WWW.
Our work aims to develop a model that accurately classifies news, in order to reveal the trending topics that disturb the citizens of a nation through a cheaper(in terms of man hours and financial resources) and faster methodology. We make a comparison between different ML algorithms. For the dataset, we use news from Ecuador, a country in means of development from Latin America.
